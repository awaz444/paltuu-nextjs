Bringing a puppy home is one of the most exciting things you can do as a pet owner in Pakistan. But the first question most new owners ask is: **what do I feed them?** Get nutrition right in the first year and you set the foundation for a healthy, energetic, long-lived dog. Get it wrong and you risk growth problems, bone issues, and a compromised immune system.

This guide covers everything specific to feeding a puppy in Pakistan — the right nutrients, the best brands available locally, feeding schedules, and what to absolutely avoid.

## Why Puppy Nutrition Matters More Than You Think

Puppies grow at a remarkable rate. A Labrador puppy, for example, goes from 500 grams at birth to 30+ kilograms in about a year. This explosive growth requires significantly more protein, fat, calcium, and phosphorus than an adult dog needs. Feeding an adult dog formula to a puppy means they are chronically undernourished even if they appear full.

Puppy-specific food is not just a marketing gimmick — it genuinely has different nutritional profiles calibrated for growth.

## Protein is the Foundation

Growing muscles need high-quality protein. The first ingredient in your puppy's food should always be a named meat source: chicken, beef, lamb, or fish. Avoid foods where the first ingredient is a grain (corn, wheat, rice) or a vague "meat meal" without specifying the animal.

**Protein percentages to look for:**
- Small and medium breeds: 22-28% protein
- Large breeds: 20-24% protein (slightly lower to prevent too-rapid growth)

Quality protein sources support muscle development, enzyme production, and immune function. Puppies that grow up on protein-deficient diets frequently develop muscle weakness and immune problems.

## Essential Fats: Omega-3 and Omega-6

Fats provide energy and help your puppy absorb fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K). But more importantly for puppies, omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids are critical for:

- **Brain development**: DHA (an omega-3) is directly linked to cognitive development and trainability
- **Eye development**: DHA supports retinal development in young animals
- **Skin and coat health**: A shiny coat and healthy skin start with good dietary fat
- **Immune system**: Omega-3s have anti-inflammatory properties that support immune health

Look for puppy foods that list fish oil or chicken fat as fat sources. Avoid foods with excessive vegetable oils as the primary fat source.

## Calcium and Phosphorus: Getting the Balance Right

Bones need calcium. But here is what most Pakistani pet owners don't know: **too much calcium is as dangerous as too little**, especially for large breed puppies. Excess calcium causes abnormally rapid bone growth, leading to skeletal problems like hip dysplasia and osteochondrosis.

- **Small breeds (under 10kg adult weight)**: Standard puppy food ratios are fine
- **Large breeds (over 25kg adult weight)**: Specifically use large breed puppy formula — it has lower calcium levels calibrated for appropriate growth rates

This is one of the most common nutritional mistakes in Pakistan, where large breeds like German Shepherds, Labradors, and Rottweilers are popular.

## Puppy Food Brands Available in Pakistan

Here are the most commonly available puppy food brands in Pakistan, ranked roughly by nutritional quality:

### Premium Tier
**Royal Canin** — Available at most pet stores in Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad. Breed-specific puppy formulas are excellent (Labrador Junior, German Shepherd Puppy, etc.). Expensive but worth it for the first year.

**Brit Care** — A Czech brand that has entered the Pakistani market. High meat content, grain-free options available. Good value for premium tier.

### Mid-Range Tier
**ProChoice** — Pakistan's own premium pet food brand. Made locally, widely available, and specifically formulated for Pakistani climate and ingredients. Puppy formula has good protein ratios.

**Pedigree Puppy** — The most widely available puppy food in Pakistan, found even in small cities. Not the highest quality but reliable, affordable, and consistent. A solid choice for budget-conscious owners.

### Things to Avoid
- Generic unnamed "puppy food" from local markets with no listed ingredients
- Human food as primary diet (common in Pakistan — rice, roti, scraps)
- Adult dog food for puppies
- Cow's milk (puppies are lactose intolerant after weaning)

## Age-Based Feeding Schedule

Puppies need more frequent meals than adult dogs because their stomachs are small but their energy demands are enormous.

| Age | Meals per Day | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 6–12 weeks | 4 meals | Small, frequent portions |
| 3–6 months | 3 meals | Larger portions per meal |
| 6–12 months | 2 meals | Transitioning toward adult schedule |
| 12+ months | 1–2 meals | Full adult portions |

Stick to consistent meal times. Consistent feeding also regulates digestion and helps with potty training — puppies typically need to go 20-30 minutes after eating.

## How Much to Feed

Every quality puppy food bag has a feeding guide on the back based on current weight and expected adult size. Follow this as your baseline, then adjust based on your puppy's body condition:

- **Too thin** (ribs easily visible): Increase by 10%
- **Just right** (ribs felt but not seen, visible waist): Maintain current amount
- **Too chunky** (ribs hard to feel, no waist definition): Decrease by 10%

Weigh your puppy weekly for the first six months. A consistent weight gain curve is a sign of healthy growth.

## Wet Food vs Dry Food (Kibble) for Puppies

Both have advantages:

**Dry food (kibble):**
- Convenient and shelf-stable
- Better for dental health (chewing action reduces tartar)
- More economical
- Easy to measure portions

**Wet food (canned/pouch):**
- Higher moisture content (helps with hydration, important in Pakistan's heat)
- More palatable for picky puppies
- Closer to natural diet in texture
- Better for puppies with dental issues or recovering from illness

Many Pakistani pet owners and vets recommend a **mixed approach**: dry kibble as the primary diet with wet food as a topper or occasional meal. This gives the dental benefits of dry food while adding variety and moisture.

## Transitioning Between Food Brands

Switching puppy food too suddenly causes digestive upset — loose stools, vomiting, and loss of appetite. Whenever changing food, transition gradually over 7-10 days:

- Days 1-3: 75% old food, 25% new food
- Days 4-6: 50% old food, 50% new food
- Days 7-9: 25% old food, 75% new food
- Day 10+: 100% new food

## What Human Foods Are Safe (and Unsafe) for Puppies

Some Pakistani household foods are fine as occasional treats. Others are dangerous.

**Safe in small amounts:**
- Plain boiled chicken (excellent protein source)
- Plain boiled rice (easy on the stomach)
- Carrots (good for teeth)
- Plain yogurt (plain dahi, in small amounts)
- Cooked egg

**Absolutely never feed:**
- Chocolate (theobromine is toxic to dogs)
- Grapes or raisins (cause kidney failure)
- Onions and garlic (toxic to dogs — common in Pakistani cooking)
- Xylitol (artificial sweetener in candies and some peanut butter)
- Cooked bones (splinter and cause intestinal perforations)
- Spiced or salted food

Many Pakistani owners unknowingly feed dangerous food thinking it is harmless. Garlic in particular is a common cause of toxicity because it is used so widely in Pakistani cooking.

## Supplements: Do Puppies Need Them?

A puppy on a complete, balanced commercial diet generally does not need supplements. Excess supplementation — especially calcium — can cause harm. Consult your vet before adding anything.

The one exception is omega-3 supplementation for puppies on lower-quality food without adequate fish oil content. A small amount of fish oil can significantly benefit brain and coat development.
