Puppies have specific dietary needs to support their rapid growth and endless energy. Learn what to feed your new best friend.

## Protein is Key

Growing muscles need high-quality protein. Look for puppy food where the first ingredient is real meat like chicken, beef, or lamb.

Quality protein sources help build strong muscles and support healthy development. Aim for puppy food with at least 22-28% protein content.

## Balanced Fats

Fats provide energy and help absorb vitamins. Omega-3 and Omega-6 fatty acids are crucial for healthy skin and a shiny coat.

These essential fatty acids also support brain development and immune function in growing puppies.

## Feeding Schedule

Puppies should eat 3-4 times a day until they are about 6 months old. Consistent feeding times help with potty training too!

### Age-Based Feeding Guide

- **6-12 weeks**: 4 meals per day
- **3-6 months**: 3 meals per day
- **6-12 months**: 2 meals per day
- **12+ months**: 1-2 meals per day

## Essential Nutrients

Beyond protein and fats, puppies need:

- **Calcium and Phosphorus**: For strong bones and teeth
- **DHA**: For brain and eye development
- **Antioxidants**: For immune system support

## What to Avoid

Never feed your puppy:

- Chocolate
- Grapes or raisins
- Onions and garlic
- Xylitol (artificial sweetener)
- Cooked bones
