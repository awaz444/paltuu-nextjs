As temperatures rise, our furry friends need extra care to stay safe and cool. Here are the top tips for summer pet safety.

## Keep Them Hydrated

Just like humans, pets can get dehydrated quickly in the heat. Ensure they always have access to fresh, cool water. Consider adding ice cubes to their water bowl for an extra treat.

## Watch the Paws

Hot pavement can burn your pet's sensitive paw pads. If it's too hot for your hand, it's too hot for their paws. Walk them early in the morning or late in the evening.

## Never Leave Them in the Car

> "Even with windows cracked, a car can become a furnace in minutes. Never leave your pet behind in a vehicle."

## Signs of Heatstroke

- Excessive panting
- Drooling
- Reddened gums
- Vomiting

If you notice any of these signs, contact your veterinarian immediately. Find certified vets near you on Paltuu's vet listings.

## Create Shaded Areas

Ensure your pet has access to shaded areas when outdoors. Consider setting up a cooling mat or a small kiddie pool for them to splash around in.

## Adjust Exercise Routines

Limit intense exercise during peak heat hours. Opt for early morning or late evening walks when temperatures are cooler.
