Cats are clean creatures, but they still need help. From brushing to nail trimming, here is how to groom your cat.

## Brushing is Bonding

Regular brushing reduces shedding and hairballs. It's also a great way to bond with your cat.

### How Often to Brush

- **Long-haired cats**: Daily brushing
- **Short-haired cats**: 2-3 times per week

Brushing helps distribute natural oils, keeping your cat's coat shiny and healthy.

## Nail Trimming

Trim your cat's nails every 2-3 weeks to prevent them from getting too sharp or growing into the paw pads.

### Nail Trimming Tips

1. Use cat-specific nail clippers
2. Trim only the sharp tip, avoiding the pink quick
3. Reward your cat with treats after each session
4. If unsure, consult a professional groomer or vet

## Bathing Your Cat

Most cats don't need frequent baths, but occasional bathing can help with:

- Removing excess oil
- Reducing allergens
- Treating skin conditions

Use cat-specific shampoo and lukewarm water. Never use human shampoo as it can irritate their skin.

## Ear Cleaning

Check your cat's ears weekly for:

- Dirt or debris
- Redness or swelling
- Unusual odor
- Excessive wax

Clean gently with a cotton ball and cat ear cleaner. Never insert anything into the ear canal.

## Dental Care

Dental hygiene is crucial for your cat's overall health:

- Brush teeth 2-3 times per week with cat toothpaste
- Provide dental treats
- Schedule annual dental checkups

## When to Seek Professional Help

Visit a professional groomer or veterinarian if:

- Your cat has severe matting
- You're uncomfortable trimming nails
- Your cat becomes aggressive during grooming
- You notice skin issues or parasites
